What to Do in an Emergency

No true plumbing emergency goes unattended. No true plumbing emergency gets scheduled days or weeks later. If you have a serious issue, we’ll be there to handle it. Usually within an hour or two.
Like our plumbers, our vans aren’t ordinary. They’re equipped with nearly every part you can imagine. And we continuously restock inventory. Sure, we make a bigger investment upfront. But we do this to save time, which means everything in an emergency. We have everything we need when we arrive.
To help get to you as fast as possible, we also use the most advanced dispatching technology in the industry. And our plumbers will verify their ETA with a call or text while en route to your home or business.
WHAT IS AN EMERGENCY? Here’s what we consider an emergency service.
Not every situation is an emergency. So, how do you know when yours is? Here are the 5 most common culprits:

  • Burst or frozen pipe
  • Water heater failure
  • Overflowing toilet
  • Drain and sewer backups
  • Leaking faucets and fixtures

If you’re not sure if you’re having a true emergency, call us.

We’ll help you figure it out.

DON’T LET THE
PROBLEM GET WORSE

If you experience a plumbing emergency, here’s what to do. In a true emergency, do these things to keep the situation from getting worse before your plumber arrives.

  1. Shut off the water to the problem fixture or house
  2. Turn off your water heater
  3. If you see a leak, wrap it with a towel and protect your walls and flooring
  4. Open the spigots outside (this will drain away any remaining water)
  5. Call your plumber immediately
